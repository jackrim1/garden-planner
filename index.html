Guiding Principles of Style

Natural, semi-wild, English country garden. Lots of whites and greens; smatterings of purple; dots of yellow and orange. Not too many reds. Plants should feel as though they have arrived of their own accord — in loose drifts and self-seeded colonies rather than regimented rows. Repetition of key plants across beds creates rhythm without uniformity.
